
# Tooltip

# USAGE GUIDELINES

- Use tooltips for quick clarification or definitions, especially for icons, abbreviations, or unclear UI labels.

- Use only for concise, non-essential information.

- Triggered by hover (desktop) or tap (mobile).

- Should not interrupt the user or require action to proceed.

- Only used when the user initiates interest by hovering or tapping.

- Avoid using tooltips on mobile unless paired with tappable icons.

- Do not use tooltips to convey important instructions or warnings.

- Use Light or Dark tooltip depending on the surrounding contrast or background complexity.

- Keep content extremely short (1–2 lines).
# CONTENT GUIDELINES

- Maximum 1–2 lines (&tilde;85 characters)

- Keep copy concise, neutral, and supportive.

- Use sentence case.

- Do not use links, actions, or long instructions.

- Avoid jargon or technical terms.

- Do not place essential or blocking information in tooltips; important content must remain visible.
# BEHAVIOUR (INTERACTIONS)

  

### Trigger
- Desktop: appears on hover or focus.
- Mobile: appears on tap; hides on tap-away.

### Placement
- Anchored to a tooltip icon or trigger element.
- Tooltip automatically adjusts placement (top, bottom, left, right, corner alignments) to avoid clipping.

### Dismissal
- Hides when hover ends (desktop).
- Hides when user taps outside (mobile).
