
# Overview

---

# OVERVIEW

Colour plays a critical role in how users understand, navigate, and interact with CAG digital products.  Within the Design Language System, colour is treated as a functional system, not a decorative layer.

The colour system is designed to:

- Communicate **meaning and status clearly**

- Support **hierarchy and focus** in dense interfaces

- Reinforce **brand recognition** without overwhelming content

- Remain** accessible** and **legible** across lighting conditions, devices, and contexts

- Scale consistently across **mobile, web, and enterprise platforms**


All colour usage within the system is intentional, constrained, and semantic&hyphen;driven to ensure clarity and consistency across the ecosystem.

---

# COLOUR ARCHITECTURE

The colour system is structured in layers to separate meaning from appearance:

1. **Foundational:** Raw colour values grouped into families (e.g. neutrals, secondary).

1. **Semantic:** Abstract meanings such as Primary, Success, Error, Disabled.

1. **Modes:** Variations for interaction states and light/dark

---

# COLOUR SYSTEM PRINCIPLES

### Consistent Across Ecosystem

Colour should communicate meaning before aesthetics. Designers should always select a semantic role, not a specific hue.

### Composable and Scalable

The system supports light and dark modes, brand evolution, and future themes without breaking semantic meaning.

Gradients may be used to:

- Add depth to hero surfaces and banner

- Support brand expression in promotional contexts

- Enhance illustrations and large background areas
